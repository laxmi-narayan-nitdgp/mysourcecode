The Six Pillars of Iman

 

Just as Islam has five pillars (please recall tohe definitions of Islam and Pillar), so Iman (please recall the definition of Iman in all of its three necessary components) also has six pillars, taught to us by the Prophet (saws).

 

(1) Belief in Allah Most High.

He is free of any need for others. He is the sole Creator of all that which pleases Him (and that which doesn't). Allah has ordered His creation and forbidden them only in ways which are in their best interest. He sent the Messengers and the books with the truth Ilm-ul-Ghaib

And there is none like unto Him."

(2) Belief in the Angels.

Belief that among Allah's creation are angels.

(3) Belief in the Books of Allah.

Six specific books mentioned in the Qur'an:

(4) Belief in the prophets.

Belief in the process of prophethood. Allah in His wisdom did not neglect His creation.

(5) Belief in the resurrection

Reconstruction of the body and return of the soul to it. People will come forth out of their graves like locusts. Faces bent down. Rushing to the Caller. [See 53/76 Al-Qamar; 70/43 Al-Ma'arij; 17/49 Al-Israa; 36/78 Yasin; 20/108 Taha] The resurrection is of the body and the soul, not some metaphysical resurrection of the latter.

(6) Belief In Predestination and Decree

A Muslim believes in Allah's predestination of all things and events (Qadhaa), His decree (Qadar), His wisdom in His actions, and His will. Nothing in the universe can occur, even the voluntary actions of His slaves, except after Allah's knowledge, and His decree of that event. A Muslim further believes that Allah is Just in His predestination and His decree, Wise in all of His actions. His wisdom follows His will: Whatever He wills is, and whatever He does not will is not.
